Important information about these policies

Here's some useful information about excesses, legal cover and roadside assistance.

Total excess

What does total excess mean in real terms?

The total excess on an insurance policy is a fixed cost you'll pay in the event of a claim, even if you're not at fault. You'll usually get this cost refunded if the claim is later found to be non fault.

Total excess is made up of:
  • Compulsory excess
  • Voluntary excess
  • Young and inexperienced driver excess (where applicable).

Here's an example of what this could look like.

Complusory excess   Voluntary excess   Total excess you'll pay
£100 + £150 = £250
Cost of damage   Total excess you'll pay   How much we'll pay
£1,000 - £250 = £750
 

Compulsory excess

What's compulsory excess?

Compulsory excess is the amount your insurer asks you to pay in the event of a claim.

Voluntary excess

What's voluntary excess?

Voluntary excess is the amount you choose to add to the compulsory excess. Including voluntary excess normally reduces your premium but you must be sure you're able to afford the total excess in the event of a claim. If you had a car repaired and couldn't pay the total excess the repairer wouldn't release the car back to you until your excess was paid. They'll probably also charge you for storing the vehicle at their premises.

You should check your excess amounts before you buy as the voluntary excess you request may not always be granted.

Young and inexperienced driver excess

What's young and inexperienced driver excess?

We add an additional excess of:

  • £295 for anyone aged 17-20
  • £195 for anyone aged 21-24
  • £195 for anyone aged over 25 who has held their licence for less than one year, or have a provisional licence.

These amounts are in addition to the other excesses shown on your schedule. This means the total excess you'd need to pay out in the event of a claim would increase by the amounts shown above if a young or inexperienced driver was using the vehicle at the time of the incident.

Legal cover

If you're involved in an accident that wasn't your fault, you're covered for up to £100,000 of legal costs to help you claim for:

  • Compensation for any injuries
  • Damage to your belongings
  • Being unable to use your vehicle
  • Recovery of any excess you've paid

Plus you'll have:

  • Access to a 24 hour helpline for advice on any private legal matter
  • Legal costs to defend yourself if you're being prosecuted over a driving offence.

There must be a 51% or higher chance of success and you may be able to arrange legal support without this policy, although this might mean you have to pay fees or a proportion of your compensation.

Roadside assistance

If your car breaks down you're covered for the engineer's call-out fees and labour costs for up to 1 hour. As long as your car's safe, roadworthy and accessible, we'll get you back on the move there and then

You're also covered for:

  • If you use the wrong fuel, cover is provided for draining, flushing and refuelling your car up to 10 litres
  • Caravans and trailers attached to your vehicle
  • Unlimited number of callouts
  • Tow to a garage or original destination if within 15 miles

What isn't covered:

  • Costs of parts and materials
  • Breakdowns within a quarter of a mile from you home address

If you'd like a higher level of cover, you can upgrade to one of our other breakdown products on the next screen.
